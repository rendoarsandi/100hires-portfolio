# Email Length and Reply Rates

**Source**: [LinkedIn Post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/willallred_coldemail-sales-copywriting)
**Date**: 2026-06-15

## Post Content

The data is clear: short emails win.

We analyzed 10 million cold emails and here's what we found:
- Emails under 50 words get a 17% reply rate.
- Emails with 50-100 words get a 12% reply rate.
- Emails over 100 words drop to under 7% reply rate.

Why?
Because people read their emails on mobile devices while doing something else.
If they see a wall of text, they archive it.

Keep it simple:
1. Hook: Mention a specific trigger (news, hire, tool).
2. Problem: Poke a known pain point.
3. Value: One line of how you solve it.
4. Call to Action: A simple low-friction question (e.g. 'Are you open to looking at the data?').

Stop pitching. Start conversation.
